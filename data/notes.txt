Title: Darbhanga — Quick Notes [web:11]

Overview:
- Location: City in Darbhanga district, Mithila region, Bihar; serves as district/divisional HQ [web:11]. 
- Status: Considered an important city in North Bihar and noted among Bihar’s larger urban centers [web:1]. 
- Setting: On/near the Baghmati River within the Gangetic plains of North India [web:4][web:8][web:14]. 
- Proximity: Roughly ~50 km from the Nepal border area (northern direction) [web:14]. 

Identity & Culture:
- Region: Core of historical Mithila with deep Janak–Vidarbha traditions and Maithili cultural heritage [web:11][web:4]. 
- Arts: Known for Mithila (Madhubani) painting, sujni embroidery, sikki crafts, and classical Dhrupad traditions [web:4]. 
- Local Specialties: Trade in fish, mango, and makhana is well known across the district [web:17]. 
- Festivals: Kartik Purnima Mela, Dussehra Mela, and Diwali Mela are notable annual events [web:17]. 

History (Very Brief):
- Seat of Darbhanga Raj; rose prominently from the 16th century as a cultural and administrative center [web:4][web:11]. 
- Administrative milestones include becoming a separate district in 1875 and a divisional HQ post-independence (1972) [web:11]. 

Getting There:
- Air: Darbhanga Airport (DBR) resumed/started commercial services on 2 Dec 2020; currently handles domestic routes (no international) [web:4][web:6]. 
- Rail: Darbhanga is a major junction within ECR (Samastipur division) with broad-gauge links to Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Guwahati, and more [web:4]. 
- Road: Connected via NH-57 and NH-105 along with state highways across North Bihar [web:4]. 

Top Sights (In/near City):
- Darbhanga Raj Complex: Nargauna Palace, Anand Bagh Palace, Bela Palace; heritage precinct with university use today [web:4]. 
- Shyama Mai (Kali) Temple: Built by Darbhanga Raj in 1933; significant local pilgrimage site [web:4]. 
- Chandradhari Museum & Maharaja Laxmishwar Singh Museum: Collections from royal and regional heritage [web:4]. 
- Kusheshwarsthan Bird Sanctuary: Seasonal wetlands across blocks; notable for migratory and local bird diversity [web:4]. 
- Ahilya Asthan (near Kamtoul): Site tied to Ramayana lore; local fairs during Ram Navami and Vivah Panchami [web:4]. 

Education & Institutions:
- Lalit Narayan Mithila University (LNMU), the central university presence in town [web:16][web:10]. 
- Kameshwar Singh Darbhanga Sanskrit University and several colleges/technical and medical institutes are based here [web:4]. 

Geography, Rivers, and Monsoon:
- Rivers: Network includes Kamla, Baghmati, Kosi, Kareh, and Adhwara groups shaping floodplains and chaurs [web:4][web:11]. 
- Rainfall & Floods: Receives most of ~1142 mm annual rainfall during monsoon; low-lying areas experience seasonal flooding [web:4][web:11]. 

Quick Orientation:
- Division: Darbhanga division includes districts Darbhanga, Madhubani, and Samastipur [web:11]. 
- Regional Position: Part of the Gangetic plains; east of the Baghmati and close to the Nepal frontier [web:8][web:14]. 
- Urban Note: Recognized as an important urban center in North Bihar’s Mithilanchal belt [web:1][web:11].
